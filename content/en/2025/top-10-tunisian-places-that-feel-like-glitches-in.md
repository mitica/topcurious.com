### 1. Chott El Jerid — The Mirror Lake That Lies

![Chott El Jerid — The Mirror Lake That Lies](/_images/f/fe83939930bf27c081d08aa2b7407e79-medium.webp)

A salt pan so wide your eyes swear it’s water, then betray you with heat-shimmered ghosts. In winter it blushes pink and tosses up islands of salt like frosted coral. Drivers aim at the horizon and end up chasing mirages. Folklore says caravans vanished here not from thirst, but from certainty they could walk on reflections.

### 2. Bulla Regia — The Roman Villas That Moved Underground

![Bulla Regia — The Roman Villas That Moved Underground](/_images/3/3740399e569a4dd416ab76727337d5aa-medium.webp)

When the sun got cruel, the Romans built their dream houses beneath the earth. You descend stairs and find mosaic dining rooms cool as a whisper. It feels like breaking into a private party paused for two millennia. The rooms are gorgeous, but the silence is thick enough to taste.

### 3. Korbus & Ain Atrous — Hot Springs That Pour Into the Sea

![Korbus & Ain Atrous — Hot Springs That Pour Into the Sea](/_images/7/701117a7586bfae85cd94cf849b2f079-medium.webp)

Steam sighs out of cliffside cracks and slips straight into the Mediterranean. Locals soak where waves and mineral heat argue over your skin. Romans did it first, Instagram did it second. On windy days the whole cove smells like a seaside potion.

### 4. Ksar Ouled Soltane — The Grain Fortress That Became Sci‑Fi

![Ksar Ouled Soltane — The Grain Fortress That Became Sci‑Fi](/_images/4/482c826dbf947953a62972c82890bfc4-medium.webp)

An anthill of arched granary cells stacks up like a beehive dreamed by an architect. Sunlight turns the mud-brick gold and the doors glow like coins. It once guarded grain; now it hoards shadows, stories, and film-nerd pilgrimages. You can almost hear the barter of centuries echoing in the courtyard.

### 5. Matmata — The Village That Lives Inside the Earth

![Matmata — The Village That Lives Inside the Earth](/_images/f/f5e00152f6b3e98c2b1fcd6e2bde467c-medium.webp)

People carved courtyards into soft rock and let the sky be their ceiling. Houses hide in the cliff, cool as a memory even at noon. Storms rearrange the edges, and families patch the earth like cloth. It’s part museum, part living secret—sci‑fi set, real life address.

### 6. Ghar El Melh — The Port That Got Trapped by Sand

![Ghar El Melh — The Port That Got Trapped by Sand](/_images/9/9dd238243aeaffa9069a7cae648db366-medium.webp)

Ottoman forts stare over a lagoon that slowly sealed its own exit. Fishermen glide on water flat as a sheet of glass while birds rehearse migration routes overhead. The place feels paused, as if the sea forgot to hit play. At dusk, the fort walls turn pink and so do the rumors.

### 7. Ichkeul — The Lake That Breathes Between Fresh and Salt

![Ichkeul — The Lake That Breathes Between Fresh and Salt](/_images/0/0f3bc7d7272f7001350851f4113e1c77-medium.webp)

Each year the lake flips its personality—sweet in winter, brinier in summer. Tens of thousands of birds treat it like an all‑inclusive layover. Water buffalo mow the marsh like slow, living lawnmowers. It’s a park that survives by changing its mind with the seasons.

### 8. El Djem — The Colosseum Nobody Expects

![El Djem — The Colosseum Nobody Expects](/_images/5/5de418782770170dc8aae362562f7510-medium.webp)

A Roman arena rises from a modest town like a memory with perfect posture. It’s huge enough to steal your breath, empty enough to keep it. The stone bleeds honey at sunset and swallows footsteps whole. Locals tell stories of rebels who used it as a fortress, and the arches still look ready for a last stand.

### 9. Kerkennah — The Archipelago That Runs on Patience

![Kerkennah — The Archipelago That Runs on Patience](/_images/6/69ced5e485f5928bd1807aa039ca8134-medium.webp)

Here the sea is shallow, the tides are shy, and time seems to nap. Fishermen weave charfiya traps in geometric patience only the ocean can read. Palms lean like they’re whispering about octopus jars and quiet deals. Visitors swear the islands slow your heartbeat to island speed.

### 10. Mahdia’s Sunken Columns — The City That Keeps Losing and Finding Itself

![Mahdia’s Sunken Columns — The City That Keeps Losing and Finding Itself](/_images/4/4def5f723a2c3fb38813d8eedb13d4f3-medium.webp)

Storms lift the lid on the seabed and reveal carved stones like teeth in a grin. Divers trace streets that slid under waves across centuries of sieges and shipwrecks. Fishermen swap rumors of anchors tangled in history. On clear days, you can stand on the cape and feel the past blinking up at you.
