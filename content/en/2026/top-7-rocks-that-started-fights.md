### 1. Mars Potato Rock

![Mars Potato Rock](/_images/e/e23a7735a2cd364834eb1994035ce532-medium.webp)

Found by researchers in Antarctica. At first mistaken for a frozen potato in a lunch cooler. Almost thrown away before testing. Later confirmed as a rare Martian meteorite worth millions.

### 2. Backyard Boundary Stone

![Backyard Boundary Stone](/_images/2/26ab4bd0fb9c5ebe371f881b5dd28e20-medium.webp)

Marked the edge of two family properties for decades. One neighbor moved it two feet during a garden project. Led to a five year court case over land deeds. The stone now sits in a museum display.

### 3. Gold Rush Claim Rock

![Gold Rush Claim Rock](/_images/a/ac0a48b350aa6cb850c87efb94ca8c22-medium.webp)

Used to stake the first claim in a California gold field. Two miners argued it belonged to each of them separately. The rock was split in half during the fight. Both halves ended up in different museums.

### 4. Radioactive Paperweight

![Radioactive Paperweight](/_images/1/18d10c13c3821278b674fdc70a47f7e2-medium.webp)

Kept on a government desk for thirty years. Workers only learned it was uranium ore after health scans. Lawsuit followed over radiation exposure at work. The rock now rests in a lead lined case.

### 5. Stolen Beach Pebble

![Stolen Beach Pebble](/_images/f/fdcf89c9aab91e1765914ce9303c9223-medium.webp)

Taken from a protected beach in Wales as a souvenir. Tourist posted photos online and faced arrest threats. Local council demanded its return after public outcry. The pebble was mailed back in a padded envelope.

### 6. Volcano Warning Stone

![Volcano Warning Stone](/_images/3/35d5f32104b91b00d7bdfa9de64cbfb5-medium.webp)

Placed near an active vent by early settlers as a danger marker. Hikers kept moving it for better photos. A tourist fell after shifting the rock and sued the park. Officials now bolt similar stones in place.

### 7. Auction House Fossil Rock

![Auction House Fossil Rock](/_images/b/b61615ef8723d35d8b07a44c90c1d536-medium.webp)

Sold as dinosaur footprint at a high price. Buyer later claimed it was only a rock pattern. Experts disagreed in public statements for months. The item stayed unsold after the legal dispute.
