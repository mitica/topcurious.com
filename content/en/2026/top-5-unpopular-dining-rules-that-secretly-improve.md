### 1. No Substitutions, No Modifications

![No Substitutions, No Modifications](/_images/4/4b32f788d9b38621820418f5af52ecdf-medium.webp)

Chefs build dishes around a precise balance that can collapse if one element changes. Special orders slow the line and knock timing off for every table. It also reduces cross-contact risks when multiple custom tickets hit at once. It feels stubborn, but the plates arrive faster and more consistent.

### 2. No Phones or Flash at the Table

![No Phones or Flash at the Table](/_images/f/ff14c17911b9ed2710ee23af9d6c28de-medium.webp)

Flash distracts nearby tables, and waiting for photos makes delicate plating wilt and sauces set. Lingering for the perfect shot turns hot dishes lukewarm and fries soggy. Some spots also ban tripods to protect staff privacy and service flow. It frustrates influencers, but flavors land at their peak.

### 3. Tiny Menu That Changes Daily

![Tiny Menu That Changes Daily](/_images/e/e931c8f8517c159ad130840db6557b89-medium.webp)

A short list lets kitchens buy peak produce and use whole animals or fish. Streamlined prep means tighter seasoning, better sears, and fewer mistakes. Fast turnover cuts waste without quietly shrinking portions. You might miss a favorite, but the hit rate on flavor jumps.

### 4. Bread and Water Only on Request

![Bread and Water Only on Request](/_images/a/a21bd7537adb5f2807ef2fdab617d572-medium.webp)

Bottomless baskets and auto-refills create huge waste and blunt the appetite. Asking first avoids misunderstandings about what’s complimentary. It also lets servers pace salt and hydration with the meal. It can feel stingy, but focus stays on the dishes you actually ordered.

### 5. No Takeout for Certain Dishes

![No Takeout for Certain Dishes](/_images/c/cbac646533c2b98b6d8a74158a424fe7-medium.webp)

Some plates depend on steam, crunch, or emulsions that collapse in boxes. Ramen, fried batters, and soufflés lose structure minutes after plating. Keeping them dine-in avoids food-safety risks when sauces sit warm too long. It seems controlling, but it prevents sad leftovers from killing the restaurant’s reputation.
